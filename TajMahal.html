Agra, India

Taj Mahal


India's Agra is home to the Taj Mahal, a marvel of elegant architecture. This magnificent marble tomb complex is a monument to the Mughal era's ability for construction, drawing the interest of visitors from all over the world. The Taj Mahal was commissioned by the bereaved Emperor Shah Jahan, who ruled from 1628 to 1658, as a touching tribute to his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, affectionately known as the "Chosen One of the Palace." Her untimely death in 1631, while giving birth to their 14th child, left Shah Jahan heartbroken and inspired him to build this enduring monument.
Its inclusion among the New Seven Wonders of the World confirms the Taj Mahal's importance on a worldwide scale. This building is a combination of exquisite workmanship and creative genius. According to legend, 20,000 workers toiled diligently on the Taj Mahal's construction under the direction of a council of architects knowledgeable about the technology available at the time. The ultimate product is a building of unrivaled beauty that combines white marble, priceless stones, and beautifully planned gardens in an elegant way.
The Taj Mahal is more than just a building; it is a representation of eternal love, a showcase for outstanding architecture, and a veritable gold mine of tradition and history. Visitors who want to be swept away into a world of classic romance and artistic magnificence continue to flock to it because of its ethereal beauty set against the Yamuna River's backdrop.
